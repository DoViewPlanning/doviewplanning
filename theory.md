#Theory

**Draft Only. Last updated 2026-03-29** The theory behind DoView Boards.

## Summary

The design of DoView Boards is conceptually grounded in DoView Planning methodology which, in turn, is grounded in outcomes theory. Outcomes theory provides the conceptual basis for thinking about, and working with outcomes systems of any type. An outcomes system is any system that: identifies, prioritizes, aligns, measures, delegates/contracts, attributes, or holds agents (human or AI) to account for outcomes of any type in any area.

### The design principles DoView Boards are based on

DoView Boards are based on the following principles:

- Where more than one agent is working on progressing an outcomes system, removing the necessity for each agent to develop an individual internal (mental model for human agents) representation of the strategy space. This is directly analogous to the use of an external thinking tool in other settings, e.g. a blueprint plan in construction and the use of a map in military planning.

- Create a single visual representation of the strategy space, which includes all of the key strategic elements that need to be taken into account when progressing any outcomes system. These elements go by various names, e.g. inputs, processes, actions, steps, outputs, outcomes, risks mitigated, opportunities taken, measures, evaluation questions, evidence taken etc.). So that decision-makers and implementors have the same single comprehensive canonical, single source of truth representation of the strategy space which assists fully informed and aligned strategizing and implementation. In many other planning, implementation and reporting approaches, some of these are represented in different visualizations or documents e.g. risks are represented in a separate risk register. 

- Keep the cognitive load for an agent working with the representation at an appropriate level for that agent, i.e. simpler for humans, more complex for AI systems.

- Ensure that the same representation can be used across all stages of planning, implementation, measurement and reporting cycle to ensure alignment and increase efficiency.

- Ensuring that the representation can accommodate differing levels of certainty across different parts of the strategy space.
